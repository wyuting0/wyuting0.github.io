SOAC'24 (Secondary One Annual Camp 2024) was an exhilarating journey that pushed the boundaries of my comfort zone, creating many challenges that would ultimately make the camp an extraordinarily fun experience.

## So It Begins: Day 1

*March 6, 2024*

Getting to Hwa Chong on that cool morning was a bit of a struggle—and my heavy, overstuffed bags seemed like they contained every single item I’d ever need during the camp. As I dragged my bag through the busy courtyard, there was a sense of resilient determination in the air — but my bags seemed to get heavier and heavier with each step.

I loaded up my bags onto the school bus, and the adventure officially kicked off with the hum of the engine. The air was filled with anticipation and excited chit-chat as we took our separate seats. The bus ride was fairly long due to the terrible traffic conditions, so I kept myself entertained by engaging in conversations with my fellow classmates.

My class arrived at the campsite nearly **90 minutes** behind schedule. As a result, we had to forgo certain activities, namely the fire drill and the introductory briefing. This deviation from the usual schedule wouldn't be uncommon for the remaining days at the camp. Consequently, we skipped directly to lunch, which actually exceeded my expectations (certainly superior to the packed meals from SOO and P5 Camp).

The activities during day 1 were fairly bland: we had some team bulding activities and prepared our song performance for the campfire on day 2. Due to time constraints and the wet weather, we had to skip the blind man's trail, which was pretty unfortunate as I was really looking forward to it. Nonetheless, we had supper and it was lights out by 10.

## Mass Game & Campfire: Day 2

The next day at SOAC'24 kicked off at 6am, when the facilitators went to our respective dormitories to wake us up. For some reason, we had an hour and a half marked as "rise and shine" on the schedule. So, after I finished brushing my teeth, I had a solid hour to have a chat with the facilitators and discuss our plans for the day.

After we had breakfast, it was time for the mass game—where we competed with other consortiums in order to obtain as many flags as possible. It was an opportunity for us to have fun while building strong bonds with others. Unfortunately, iSpark ended up with the least flags: a measly total of **9**. I admit it was a bit embarrassing, but we gave it our all, and that's what counts.

Afterwards, it was finally time for the most anticipated and hyped-up activity of the entire camp: the campfire. Amusingly, we kicked things off by singing the "rain, rain, go away" nursery rhyme as there was already a light drizzle. As the sun began to set, each class took their turns to showcase their song or dance, creating an incredibly enjoyable evening where we all sang along to a variety of hits. 

![Campfire](https://github.com/wyuting0/wyuting0.github.io/blob/main/public/images/campfire.jpg?raw=true "Campfire")

## Strike Camp: Day 3

The the 3rd and final day at SOAC'24 started at 5am, when the facilitators, once again, went to our respective dormitories to wake us up. This time, we only has half an hour to get ready for the day. As I had already packed my bags the previous night, I did not need to do so. However, my other classmates spent their morning packing their bags. I was assigned the task of bring a trash bag to each dormitory to collect their trash. This process took around 30 minutes and was incredibly boring.

Now it's time for the real part of strike camp: cleaning the toilets. The class had to use hoses to remove dirt from the floor and sweep up any scrap twigs and leaves. Additionally, we had to pick up trash on the floor in the cubicles with our **bare hands**. Let's just say nobody was pleased with the situation. We all heaved a sigh of relief when it was finally over.

Afterwards, we all garthered at the field for a debrief and photo-taking, marking our final moments at the campsite. Once it concluded, the class boarded the bus, albeit after a lengthy 15-minute wait, and finally departed from the campsite, heading back to Hwa Chong for dismissal.

## Takeaways

SOAC'24 tested not only my physical ability but also my teamwork and adaptability. Even though it was not a comfortable experience, I knew it was never meant to be. The cramped sleeping and surprise tasks were meant to make us forge better friendships as we were all on the same boat, in the same situation. Instead of breaking us, those challenges became the glue that stuck us together, showing that facing tough times as a team builds stronger connections.
