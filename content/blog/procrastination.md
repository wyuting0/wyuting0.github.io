Today, I want to draw your attention to one personal challenge that many of us struggle with – an invisible force that causes people a lot of harm – procrastination. For long years, this darkness felt like a tight grip and I couldn’t get rid of it. I watched the reality happen and my dreams go away like sand through my fingers. It was really hard process of self-reflection and then severely strong criticism to finally escape from it. It would turn out to be the most significant and influential phase of self-betterment, ultimately molding my future in a positive way.

## Laziness is a Thief

Laziness is not harmless; it is a thief that takes your time, energy, and potential – all while you sleep having the false idea of comfort. It might make you feel that putting things off is a comfortable thing to do, as it serves as a distraction from the pressures of a hectic world. Just as I, there was a time when I started believing that this illusion was true, and my comfort zone becoming a prison bit by bit was totally unimaginable.

With my laziness spreading its tentacles into my aspirations, I could only look at myself as the master of excuses. My failed attempts at running were often said in a tone of “I’ll start tomorrow,” which was a soothing balm for the guilty feelings that accompanied my missed opportunities. Without my notice, the thief was stealing the lovely hours from me that couldn’t be replaced.

One dream after another started to pile up dust on the shelf of my dreams not yet accomplished. The books I swore I would write are yet to be written and the skills I tried to learn remain untapped, buried under tons of indolence. Being lazy didn't just cost me what I had now, it was also robbing me of a future, which could have been filled with accomplishments and growth. Every day, I neglected to put in the effort to outperform my peers. I believed that being average, like everyone else, was sufficient. 

The cunning nature of the thief is that it can wear a mask to seem as a friend. It whispers to you sweet nothing, but it pretends that you can have a couple of minutes of a good rest that a better day is coming tomorrow. However, these stolen moments create a longing in your heart, which is the depth between the place you are and the place that you could have been.

The fact flashed in my mind like lightning- it wasn't just a passive weakness, it was rather the force energetically opposing to my future. It was the right time to peep into the thief within, face the uneasy and brutal truth that I was willingly giving away my dreams to the clutches of inactivity.

In recognizing the thief, I was on the first step to redemption. I came to the understanding that laziness is something more than a simple desire to change; it is something that one has to work and try to take back what has been lost for a long time – your time, your energy, and your dreams. Escape from this plundering man took me the decision to take back every stolen instant and turn them into the future that will be brighter and more fulfilling.

## Taking Control

It wasn’t until I reached the bottom that I realized I understood how lazy took a toll on my life. I stared at my reflection in the mirror and realized that the result was the outcome of my own decisions – unrealized dreams, unachieved potential, and the heavy burden of regret. This is where I was – just another 12-year-old boy, with nothing special outside of academics. I knew I had to stop being the basic broad that I was. This moment was the beginning of a fundamental change. 

The choice to free myself from the lethargy that was holding me back was not easy. It pushed me out of my comfort zone and made me question conventional ways. I began to set realistic goals, no more being overwhelmed by the grandeur of my dreams. Small, incremental steps created the foundation of a new beginning. 

However, it wasn't only about becoming less lazy; it was about becoming more. Working harder, more disciplined, and more responsible. I have accepted the idea of delayed gratification, knowing that the denial of short-term comfort can lead to long-term gains. The mirror no longer reflected my regrets; it had become my creative outlet, a daily reminder of the person I was becoming. I wanted to become the cool kid, maintaining a flawless balance among my interests, social life, and academics.

To get out of the snare of laziness, I needed more than just a change in mindset; I needed specific actions that adhered to my newly realized desire to take the steering wheel of my life. While breaking the walls of excuses, I tried many activities that not only were challenging but also brought happiness and satisfaction into my life.

One example of this would be blogging. Writing down my thoughts was a form of self-release, a way to map my journey, and to give hope to others who were facing similar challenges. Blogging was not just a creative outlet anymore; it became a platform for responsibility. Sharing my experiences and goals with a watching audience became a driving force, making me stay consistent and committed to the journey of self-improvement.

## Changing

Little by little, slowly but surely, I was regaining control of my life. I have made my aspirations attainable objectives and have drawn up a plan that turns my dreams into measurable milestones. As I achieved more and more, the more I craved for more, and step by step, my laziness started to loosen its grip. I learned that the contrary of laziness is not only hard work; it is passion, purpose, and a flame of desire to spend every minute wisely. I began to substitute aimless scrolling with set actions that were in line with my objectives. It wasn’t always easy, but the more I believed in my dreams, the more they flourished.

As I peeled of the layers of laziness, a part of me that was long gone resurfaced. There sprung out from beneath the plethora of excuses and laziness the energetic, brave spirit, ready to welcome life’s challenges. I discovered joy in the process, and each step along the way energized my determination to continue. This significantly contributed to my success in the PSLE last year, enabling me to dedicate myself to diligent study and problem-solving. This improvement extended to my social life, turning me from a timid and shy kid into a better, more confident version of myself.

## Conclusion

If you're reading this and finding yourself nodding along, it's time for **you** to cut the ties of laziness. Your dreams are waiting for you, and every second you spend postponing is the second lost. Begin with that first step, no matter how small, and observe how the haze of laziness lifts off, revealing the path to a life full of meaning and goal achievement. I promise you won't regret it.
