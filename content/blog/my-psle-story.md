Picture this: a stressed-out kid drowning in a sea of textbooks, trying to memorise formulae and dates. Yes, that was me. The anxiety leading up to the PSLE was real. From late-night cram sessions to caffeine-fuelled study marathons, I tried it all.

Now, let's rewind a bit to the chaos that led to my anxiety build-up. Enter the realm of multiple tuitions and mountains of homework. My parents, in their well-intentioned pursuit of ensuring I had every tool for success, signed me up for what felt like a never-ending marathon of classes.

From math to science, English to Chinese, my schedule resembled a masterclass in juggling commitments. Each tuition came with its set of textbooks, worksheets, and additional homework. Before I knew it, I found myself drowning in a sea of assignments, struggling to keep my head above water. Not only that, weekends became synonymous with study marathons rather than relaxation. In fact, I had 10 hours of tuition on Saturday.

The long hours spent in tuition centres blurred into each other, leaving me with little time for anything else. No more Minecraft, and no more time to write recreationally.

The pressure to excel in each subject became overwhelming. What started as a well-intentioned effort to prepare me for the PSLE had inadvertently turned into a recipe for burnout. I felt like a robot programmed to study, ticking off tasks from a never-ending to-do list. To be brutally honest, I didn't even feel like I learnt much during this period.

So, there I was, buried under a mountain of textbooks and drowning in assignments. But, you know what they say, *necessity is the mother of invention*. In my case, it was the necessity to maintain sanity in the midst of chaos.

I stumbled upon a game-changer—effective time management. Instead of mindlessly putting in endless hours, I started breaking down my study sessions into manageable chunks. A walk around the block, a cup of tea, or even just staring into space for a few minutes became my secret sauce. Sometimes, it's better to not leave all the relaxation to the end of the study session.

Turns out, a well-placed break is like hitting the reset button for your brain. It not only improved my mood but also made my study sessions way more effective.

But, let's not forget the real hero here: ~~me~~ perseverance. Every tough topic conquered and every setback faced head-on was a small victory. The PSLE became more than just an exam; it became a test of how much I could handle and how far I could push myself.

Sure, it wasn't always smooth sailing. There were moments when giving up felt like the easiest option. But, you know what? I held on. I reminded myself that the journey itself was a win. Every hiccough, every late-night cram session, and every confusing Higher Chinese question were stepping stones toward personal growth.

Walking into the exam hall was like a mix of excitement and nerves all squished into one giant ball. It felt like time had slowed down. The questions on the paper stared back at me, kind of like old friends but with a bit of a twist. When the clock hit zero, I put down my pencil, feeling a mix of "Phew, it's over!" and "I hope I nailed it." And with that, I walked out of there with mixed emotions.

So, here comes the part I didn't see coming—the results day. I anxiously glanced at the results slip, crossing my fingers for that coveted AL4D, only to face the stark truth of an AL6D: an AL1 in all subjects except Science, where I scored an AL3, and a distinction achievement in Higher Chinese. Yep, I know, a bit of a plot twist. I won't lie; disappointment crept in, and it felt like a balloon deflating. I had dreamt of acing it, but here I was with a different outcome. Yet, in the midst of that letdown, I couldn't ignore the journey that led me here.

Looking back, it wasn't just about the grades. It was about conquering burnout, learning to manage time, and finding strength in perseverance. The PSLE became more than a test—it was a chapter of growth, resilience, and unexpected lessons. That AL6D didn't define me; it was just a small part of the bigger story.

Even though I didn't quite make it into my dream school this time around, I've come to realise that the journey is just as important as the destination. While it might be a little disappointing not to see that dream fulfilled immediately, I understand that success isn't solely defined by the school we attend. Instead, I'm choosing to appreciate the learning experiences, challenges, and personal growth that come with the academic journey. It's not about fixating on one specific school; it's about the effort and dedication I put into my studies that will ultimately shape my success. This setback is just a small part of the bigger picture, motivating me to keep working hard and believing that the lessons learnt along the way will contribute to a more significant achievement in the future.

As I bid farewell to my PSLE chapter, I carry not just a report card but a backpack full of experiences. Disappointment is real, but so is the strength found in setbacks. I'm ready for the next adventure, armed with resilience, a dash of optimism, and the knowledge that life's journey is full of surprises.

And so, my PSLE journey concludes—a tale of twists, turns, and unexpected endings. What's next? Well, the adventure continues, and I'm ready for whatever plot twists life throws my way in Hwa Chong Institution. Thanks for joining me on this ride!

*Special thanks to my amazing Primary School friends and teachers—your support will not be forgotten, and I'm truly grateful for all your help!*
